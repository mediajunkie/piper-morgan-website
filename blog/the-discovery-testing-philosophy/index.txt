Test against requirements. Verify expected behavior. Confirm implementation matches design.</p><p><strong>Discovery testing</strong>: Find out what actually exists. No assumptions about completeness. No expectations about functionality. Assumptions diverge from truth.</p><p>You can’t validate against outdated specifications. [FUTURE] items are transparency.”</em></blockquote><p name="6c67" id="6c67" class="graf graf--p graf-after--blockquote">The prompt included:</p><ul class="postList"><li name="d6d5" id="d6d5" class="graf graf--li graf-after--p">Chrome MCP commands for every interaction (screenshots, form fills, console inspection)</li><li name="7852" id="7852" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li">Evidence collection requirements (not just “it worked” but “here’s proof”)</li><li name="b379" id="b379" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li">Specific scenarios with concrete steps (not vague “test login” but exact sequence)</li><li name="c343" id="c343" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li">Expected outcomes with alternatives (not binary pass/fail but “discover what happens”)</li></ul><h3 name="9ffd" id="9ffd" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--li">The 91/93 confidence foundation</h3><p name="11d5" id="11d5" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">Sunday morning, 10:43 AM.
