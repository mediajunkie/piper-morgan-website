You can’t delegate effectively without foundations to delegate against. The preparation matters.</p><p name="a020" id="a020" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The partnership is specific: I operate at highest thinking level (strategy, vision, priorities). AI operates at execution level (implementation, verification, documentation). Together we achieve what neither could alone.</p><p name="4ad8" id="4ad8" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">My “extraordinarily light” cognitive load isn’t because I’m doing less important work. It’s because I’m doing different work — the uniquely human work of judgment, strategy, and vision — while AI handles the execution work it does systematically.</p><h3 name="ce5a" id="ce5a" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Getting to light cognitive load</h3><p name="9689" id="9689" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">The question readers might have: How do you build toward this?</p><p name="0c22" id="0c22" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Based on what made Tuesday possible, here’s what seems to matter:</p><p name="568b" id="568b" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Start with patterns. Let AI apply them systematically.</p><p name="9721" id="9721" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Build quality gates. Can’t trust execution without automatic validation. Pre-commit hooks, CI/CD, test suites — create these to verify work without manual review. Trust the gates, review results.</p><p name="2a14" id="2a14" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Document methodology. Can’t maintain consistency without clear process. Inchworm, Time Lord, Phase structure — codify approaches so AI can follow systematically. Methodology enables reliable delegation.</p><p name="6f75" id="6f75" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Create infrastructure. Can’t verify completion without comprehensive validation. Tests, documentation, metrics — build infrastructure that reveals state clearly. Verification becomes reading results, not reviewing implementations.</p><p name="d6fb" id="d6fb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">These take time. Weeks or months depending on project scale. But they compound.</p><p name="3813" id="3813" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Early investment in patterns pays back every time AI applies them. Early investment in methodology pays back every time it guides execution without intervention.</p><p name="e6de" id="e6de" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">The cognitive load becomes light not on Day 1, but on Day N after foundations enable systematic delegation.</p><h3 name="fbd0" id="fbd0" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">The strategic work that remains</h3><p name="06e9" id="06e9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">When cognitive load is “extraordinarily light,” what fills that freed mental space?</p><p name="abe9" id="abe9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Tuesday’s examples:</p><p name="b5ef" id="b5ef" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Process improvement identification: Noticing pre-commit double-commit friction.
