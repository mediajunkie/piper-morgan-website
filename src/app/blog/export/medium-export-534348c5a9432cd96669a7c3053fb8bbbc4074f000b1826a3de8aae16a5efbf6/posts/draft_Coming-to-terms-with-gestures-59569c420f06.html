Touch, turn, drag, done

with ‘gesture first’

Something huge happened when touch-based interfaces became the norm. It opened up a whole world of possibilities to make interactions faster, more natural, based more directly on the way we think and do things in the real world:

If I can see it, I might be able to reach out and touch it. If I can touch it, I should be able to do things to it, such as take it, remove it, examine it, change it, etc.

For us this starts with gestures, with a ‘gesture first’ approach.

What we mean when we say ‘gesture first’

With CloudOn you can create, edit, style, and share docs just by touching and gesturing. As we consider the many different aspects of working on docs, from creating to reviewing and sharing them, we ask ourselves first if there isn’t a way to make this available as a natural gesture.

Taking this approach, we’ve been able to shed (or, really, avoid building in the first place) hundreds of dialogs and confirmations and other interface crutches. Our controls stay out of the way until needed, appear in context and at your fingertips, and respond to gestures in consistent ways that help you learn what else you can do.

To be honest, we have only just begun to deliver on this approach, but I can share a few of the design choices we’ve made in our “gesture map” to illustrate how we apply this thinking.

The plain page metaphor

First of all, it must be said that CloudOn offers the largest document area of any editor on the market. (We know, we measured them, and we made this a top priority.) OK, but so what? Well, one big idea is that we’re going back to the cleanliness and simplicity of plain piece of paper.

The page is in front of you. What you put in on it, what you do with it, what you have to say and how. That’s all you. Our job is to get out of your way, until you need something.

We killed the Ribbon. File menu bars (glorified or otherwise) dont work when your fingers tend to be down neat he bottom of a touch screen or typing on a keyboard. When you need a tool, you can bring it up by touching an object or with our lean, efficient custom toolbars.

Similarly, Undo is there when you need it, not when you don’t.

Cut and paste was cool in its day, and then drag and drop felt a lot smoothe, but it still uses pointing, clicking, floating arrows and so on. We think it’s way easier to select some text and then drag it to a new location with your finger.

More to come

We’ll keep working to improve and extend this approach, tackling additional objects that are useful in documents (such as tables), and well as bringing that same at-your-fingertips ease to review and discussion of work in progress.

Gesture first

As a rule, when we are considering how to extend or improve the functionality of CloudOn, we think of gestures first. If there is any possible way to provide the feature in context, immediate (on touch), and through gestures (with minimal context shifting) then we will do it that way. Over time, we hope you won’t even notice.
